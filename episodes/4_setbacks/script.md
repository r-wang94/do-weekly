# Week 4: Setbacks

---

Hello and welcome to Do Health Weekly. I'm Dr Robin Wang and this week, we're talking about setbacks.

In the fifteenth century, a Japanese shogun called Ashikaga Yoshimasa broke his favourite tea bowl. He sent it away to be repaired. It came back held together with big metal staples. And you'd think that would be the end of it. But something strange happened. The tea masters who saw it didn't think the staples ruined the bowl. They thought the staples gave it something a perfect bowl could never have. A history. Proof that it had served a fine purpose, been broken, and was still here. The bowl became famous. People came to see it specifically because it had been broken and put back together. And over the centuries that followed, Japanese craftsmen took that idea even further. They started filling cracks in broken pottery with gold. They called it kintsugi. Golden repair. The broken version was worth more than the original.

We don't treat ourselves that way. When something cracks, when the week falls apart, we don't see evidence of a life being lived. We see evidence of a life going wrong. "I always do this." "I can't stick to anything." The missed walk becomes proof. The bad week becomes a life sentence. And once it's a life sentence, we often stop trying. We don't always decide to quit. Sometimes we just stop starting.

That's what setbacks really do. They don't just pause progress. They make us pretend we were never progressing.

I know what that feels like. When I was in medical school, I was obsessed with rowing. Twice a day, six times a week. I injured my back really badly. What we thought was a sports injury turned out to be a spine deformity that had been there all along, but nobody had found it. I ended up in hospital. I was supposed to be there as a student, not as a patient. I couldn't move for weeks. And I remember lying there, not asking deep questions, not having some moment of clarity about how I might go on to talk about this in a podcast. I remember just thinking, this sucks. That's about as deep as it got.

We've all had something set us back. The bad week, the lost month, the thing that knocked us sideways. Sometimes, not always, we picked ourselves back up and kept going. That's the part we forget. We just don't count the comebacks as often as we count the setbacks.

Nothing is ever erased. The walks still happened. The early nights still counted. The weeks of showing up didn't vanish because one week didn't go to plan. The Japanese craftsmen understood something we keep forgetting. The crack is not the end of the story. It's where the gold goes. Not some future version of us who always gets it right. This version. The one who showed up even when it felt pointless.

So this week, when something doesn't go to plan, and the judgement starts writing itself. 

Just do one thing that moves you forward. One walk. One early night. One meal you actually meant to cook. Not to fix anything. Just to prove the judgement wrong. Just to show to yourself that you're moving forward.

The one who kept showing up even when it felt pointless. That's not the broken version. That's the gold.

So this week... is the week of setbacks.

Thanks for being here. I'll talk to you next week.
